Sigmund Freud

Background

Sigmund Freud was a famous Austrian Neurologist born on 6 May 1856 in Freiberg in the Austrian Empire (now Příbor in the Czech Republic) and died on 23 September 1939 in London. Freud was famous for founding psychoanalysis and developing theories in human behavior, sexuality, and psychology, notable ones include those of the id, ego, and the superego, repression, and the concept of a mental defense mechanism. Though most of his theories and ideas are irrelevent in the modern era, he was still influential in the fields of psychology and neurology.

Drugs used

Sigmund Freud used tobacco and cocaine throughout his life

Cocaine:

Cocaine is an addictive drug that produces a euphoric effect when taken. Sigmund Freud himself to conducted research with his paper Über Coca, (english: On Cocaine). Chronic use of Cocaine may result in an increased rate of strokes, reduced attention span, insomnia, and other ailments

Tobacco:

Tobacco is a plant with stimulating properties that can be smoked to release dopamine and endorphines. Smoking tobacco is known to cause lung cancer, increase heart attack risk, stroke risk, and COPD. Nicotine is also highly addictive.
